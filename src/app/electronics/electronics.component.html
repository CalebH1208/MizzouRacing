Electronics

What We Do...

The Electronics Team is responsible for the design, manufacturing, testing, and integration of all electronic modules across the internal combustion and electric cars. An in-house custom wiring harness connects modules such as a MoTeC M150 ECU, custom Power Distribution Module, custom Wireless Live Telemetry, and Raspberry Pi Dashboard, among other components. The electrical components on each car provide necessary control systems for cooling, ignition, fuel, shiting, power limiting, and data acquisition.

Sub Team Lead: Jack Fels

Jack is a junior electrical engineering student and Chief Electrical Engineer for Mizzou Racing. He joined the team in the fall of 2022 and is a key member in the IC and EV builds. Jacks goals for the season are: to design both a Low Voltage and High Voltage BMS, build custom Li-Ion battery modules, make improvements to the electric powertrain, maximize cooling system power efficiency, and make reliability improvements across-the-board.

High Voltage

What We Do...

The High Voltage team is responsible for the EV powertrain and supporting electronics. The EV powertrain consists of an Accumulator-the system’s high voltage battery pack, the Traction Inverter, and the Drive Motor. The Accumulator stores 7.249kWh of energy at 480V, while being able to source 315A of DC current, all from 18650 Li-Ion cells. While competition limits us to 80kW, our system can provide up to 110kW - 147HP and 170 lb-ft of torque at the motor.
